General Info

Raleigh is the capital of North Carolina, a planned city founded and incorporated in 1792 in the state’s Piedmont region. It is an urban center and the principal city of the Raleigh–Cary metropolitan area in the Research Triangle.

Quick facts (with sources)

  • Incorporated: 1792 (planned capital named for Sir Walter Raleigh) — source: City and state history overviews and NCpedia.
  • Region: Piedmont of North Carolina (between the Coastal Plain and the Mountains) — source: NCpedia.
  • Classification: Urban city; principal city of the Raleigh–Cary, NC metropolitan statistical area — sources: U.S. Census/OMB metropolitan definitions.
  • Income vs state: Raleigh’s median household income is higher than the North Carolina median (see details below).

Population

Raleigh’s population is approximately 482,425 (ACS 2023), continuing a long pattern of steady growth as part of the Research Triangle metropolitan area.

Context and recent trend

  • Raleigh remains among the fastest-growing large cities in the Southeast, supported by tech, life sciences, government, and higher education.
  • The broader Raleigh–Cary metro is about 1.45 million people (2023 estimate) and has grown year over year in the early 2020s.
  • Growth has been concentrated around Wake County suburbs and the urban core, consistent with state trends toward metro counties.

Sources: Data USA; Neilsberg (trend).

How population estimates are produced (ordered list)

  1. Decennial census establishes a baseline count every 10 years.
  2. Post-censal estimates add/subtract births, deaths, and migration using administrative records and surveys.
  3. The American Community Survey (ACS) provides annual sample-based estimates with margins of error.

Source: U.S. Census Bureau methodology overview.

Average Salary and Income Comparison

Although average salary varies by occupation and data source, a consistent point of comparison is median household income.

Raleigh vs. North Carolina

Median household income (Raleigh, 2023 ACS via Data USA): $96,066. Statewide median household income (North Carolina, 2023 ACS): ~$71,700. Raleigh’s median is about one-third higher than the statewide figure.

Sources: Data USA: Raleigh; Census Reporter: North Carolina.

Cost of Living

Raleigh’s cost of living typically tracks near or slightly above the U.S. average and varies by neighborhood and household size. Housing costs are the largest driver, followed by transportation and childcare for many families.

  • Housing: Rapid growth has increased demand; prices and rents vary widely across Wake County.
  • Transportation: Many residents commute by car; transit options are expanding within Wake and nearby counties.
  • Everyday costs: Groceries, utilities, and healthcare indices tend to be near national averages but can fluctuate.

Weather

Raleigh’s climate features hot, humid summers and mild winters, typical of the inland Piedmont. Conditions vary year to year, but climate normals provide a reliable baseline.

  • Average January high: around 50°F (10°C); average January low: around 30–32°F (-1 to 0°C).
  • Average July high: around 90°F (32°C); average July low: around 70°F (21°C).
  • Annual precipitation: roughly 45–47 inches (1,140–1,195 mm), spread through the year.

Source: NOAA/NCEI Climate Normals for Raleigh–Durham International (1991–2020). See NWS Raleigh climate page.
