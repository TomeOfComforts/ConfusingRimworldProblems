Three things to know about Greenville’s growth

  1. It’s a regional hub for education and health care (East Carolina University and ECU Health Medical Center).
  2. Population has trended upward since 2000, with steady growth post-2020 census toward ~90k+ residents.
  3. Surrounding Pitt County remains more suburban/rural, with Greenville serving as the urban employment and services center.

Average Salary and Income Comparison

Comparing Greenville’s income to the statewide figures:

  • Greenville median household income: roughly $40k–$41k (ACS 2018–2022 range, 2022 dollars).
  • North Carolina median household income: $67,516 (ACS 2018–2022, 2022 dollars).
  • Conclusion: Greenville’s average income level is notably below the state median, consistent with college-town demographics and a large student population.

Weather and Climate

Greenville has a humid subtropical climate typical of Eastern North Carolina.

  • Summers: Warm to hot; July average highs around upper 80s to ~90°F (31–32°C).
  • Winters: Mild; January average highs ~50–53°F (10–12°C), lows near freezing.
  • Precipitation: Roughly 45–50 inches annually; occasional tropical systems in late summer/early fall.

Source: NOAA/NCEI 1991–2020 climate normals (Pitt–Greenville Airport) and state climatology summaries.

Context and Background

Greenville was chartered in 1771 as Martinsborough and renamed in 1786 to honor Revolutionary War General Nathanael Greene. Today it serves as the urban heart of the Greenville, NC Metropolitan Statistical Area, with health care and higher education as cornerstone sectors.

  1. Founded/chartered in colonial era (1771) with a subsequent relocation/renaming in 1786.
  2. Evolved into a regional medical and education hub (ECU, ECU Health Medical Center).
  3. Identified within North Carolina’s Coastal Plain/Inner Banks subregion.
