General Info

Winston-Salem is a major city in North Carolina’s northwestern Piedmont, anchored in the Piedmont Triad metropolitan region. It is best known for its Moravian heritage in Old Salem, its role in tobacco and manufacturing history, and as the home of Wake Forest University and a growing innovation economy.

Incorporation year

1913 (official merger of the towns of Winston and Salem)

Region of the state

Piedmont Triad (northwestern Piedmont of North Carolina)

City classification

Predominantly urban (core city of the Winston-Salem, NC Metropolitan Statistical Area) with surrounding suburban neighborhoods.

Historic milestones (ordered)

  1. 1766: Salem established by Moravian settlers.
  2. 1849–1859: Winston established as Forsyth County seat and incorporated.
  3. 1913: Winston and Salem officially merge as Winston-Salem.

Population

As of the latest U.S. Census Bureau estimate (July 1, 2023), Winston-Salem’s population is approximately 252,000, continuing steady growth since the 2020 decennial census count of 249,545. Source: U.S. Census Bureau QuickFacts.

­

Average Salary

Using the American Community Survey’s median household income (in 2023 dollars, 2019–2023 5-year estimate):

  • Winston-Salem median household income: roughly mid-$50,000s.
  • North Carolina statewide median household income: roughly upper-$60,000s.

That places Winston-Salem’s typical household income below the statewide median by around 15–20%, reflecting a cost-of-living-adjusted urban economy with a mix of education, healthcare, manufacturing, and services. Sources: QuickFacts — Winston-Salem, QuickFacts — North Carolina.

Cost of Living

Winston-Salem’s cost of living is typically lower than larger NC metros such as Charlotte or Raleigh, particularly in housing. Third-party indices vary by methodology, but most rate the city modestly below the national average.

For current comparisons by category (housing, groceries, transportation), see: BestPlaces: Cost of Living — Winston-Salem.

Weather

The city has a humid subtropical climate typical of the North Carolina Piedmont: four distinct seasons, hot and humid summers, and cool winters with occasional wintry precipitation. Typical summer daytime highs reach the upper 80s to low 90s °F; winter nighttime lows often dip into the low-to-mid 30s °F. Reference: WeatherSpark — Average Weather in Winston-Salem.
